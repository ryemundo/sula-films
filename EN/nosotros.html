About Us

SULA FILMS

Audiovisual production company that formally began operations in April 2009 in the city of Hermosillo, Sonora, focused on producing quality audiovisual content with universal values. Unique, marketable stories conceived under an authorial vision.

Throughout its ten-year history, it has benefited from government incentives and funds for the production and distribution of its films and educational initiatives, as well as support and invitations through PROMéxico to represent the creative industries sector in international markets and missions. Its productions have been selected for the most important festivals abroad and have been successfully screened in Mexican movie theaters.

We are a member of the Chamber of the Film Industry (CANACINE), The National Association of Latino Independent Producers (NALIP) in the United States, whose mission is to discover, promote, and inspire Latin content creators across diverse platforms, is also Mexico's representative in the International Quorum of Motion Picture Producers, made up of 100 exclusive members across five continents. This unique networking association is characterized by its membership of audiovisual production company owners from around the world, with only one representative from each sector in the country. Sulafilms' headquarters are currently located in Mexico City.

FESTIVALS

Alejandro Sugich López-Arias

Alejandro Sugich / CEO Sula Entertainment: Producer, Director, Writer. Alejandro founded the film production company Sulafilms in 2008, the film and VR distribution company Neverlanding Pictures in 2014 and the same year he co-founded Sumo Studio, a 360 and Virtual Reality production company. In 2021 established Sula Entertainment, a holding company that seeks to unify its entertainment companies into one. Sugich is a member of Nalip, Canacine and he is the current Entertainment Vice President of the International Quorum of Motion Picture Producers (IQfilm.org).

Alejandro Sugich is a Producer, Director and Writer known for Casi Treinta (2014), Los Jefes (2015), Prometo no Enamorarme (2017), Menéndez: El día del Señor (2020) and Los Hermanos Salvador (2022).
